Indicators

Indicators are tools that help you read the market more clearly. They take price data and turn it into insights that can guide your decisions. Think of them as assistants — they don’t tell you exactly buy or sell. Instead, use the data already available in the market and turn it into something that gives you an idea of the next probable move.

💡 Indicators are helpers, not decision-makers. Always trust price action first, then use indicators for confirmation.

📉 RSI (Relative Strength Index)

RSI (Relative Strength Index) tells you if a market has moved too far in one direction. It helps you spot when a price might slow down or reverse.

  • Above 70 → The market may be overbought (price moved up too fast, possible pullback)
  • Below 30 → The market may be oversold (price dropped too fast, possible bounce)
📊 RSI doesn’t guarantee a reversal — it only warns that the market is stretched.

📊 Moving Averages

Moving Averages (MA) simplify price movement by removing noise. They help you clearly see the overall direction of the market.

  • Price above MA → Buyers are in control (uptrend)
  • Price below MA → Sellers are in control (downtrend)
💡 The 50 MA shows short-to-mid trend, while the 200 MA shows the long-term trend.

📈 MACD

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) helps you understand momentum — how strong a trend is and when it might change. It compares two moving averages and shows their relationship.

📊 When MACD lines cross, it can signal a possible trend shift — but always confirm with price.

⚠️ Important Rule

Don’t overload your chart with too many indicators. Instead, pick one and really understand what it shows and why it shows it. Keeping your chart clean will help you stay consistent and win over the long run.

🚫 Keep it simple — 1 or 2 indicators are more than enough if you understand them well.

🎯 Best Practice

Indicators work best when combined with core trading concepts:

  • Candlesticks
  • Support & Resistance
  • Trend direction
🔥 The best traders don’t rely on indicators alone — they combine them with structure, trend, and logic. Some don't even use them and are still profitable!
